About the Oklahoma City Thunder

The Oklahoma City Thunder are an American professional basketball team based in Oklahoma City. The Thunder compete in the National Basketball Association (NBA) as a member of the Western Conference Northwest Division. The team plays its home games at Paycom Center.

The Thunder's NBA history began in 2008 when the Seattle SuperSonics relocated to Oklahoma City. The team was originally established as the Seattle SuperSonics in 1967. In their second season in Oklahoma City, the Thunder qualified for the playoffs for the first time since 2005. They won their first division title as the Thunder in 2011, and their first Western Conference championship in 2012, eventually losing to the Miami Heat in the NBA Finals.

The team's colors are blue, red, and yellow. The Thunder have developed rivalries with other Western Conference teams, particularly the Golden State Warriors, San Antonio Spurs, and Houston Rockets. The team is known for its passionate fan base and loud home court atmosphere.
